I was quoted in a Computer Weekly article by Nicholas Fearn examining how facial recognition technology threatens basic privacy rights. As adoption of facial recognition systems continues to grow worldwide, there is increasing concern that this technology could undermine fundamental privacy rights.

The article explores how facial recognition can be kept in check and what safeguards are needed to balance innovation with privacy protection.
